Tommy McHugh

Undergraduate Researcher

Tommy McHugh is a Learning Sciences and Computer Science undergraduate student at Northwestern University. He studies the design of technologies for accessibility, learning, and creativity. Recently, his research has explored how to empower students and content professionals with disabilities to build their own assistive technologies and how to design inclusive computional learning experiences through the creation of accessible digital media.

His work has received awards by the ACM and Microsoft and he has previously worked at Google Research. Tommy currently works at the Northwestern Inclusive Technology Lab and is advised by Professor Anne Marie Piper. His work is currently funded in part by NSF Grant IIS-1901456.

Want to learn more? Shoot him a note at mchugh@u.northwestern.edu.
